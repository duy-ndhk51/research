## Business Impact
- 🚀 **Chart of accounts reverted to account module** — accountants can now find the chart of accounts where they naturally expect it (in the account section), instead of hunting for it inside the trial balance tab. This fixes a UX regression that was causing confusion in daily accounting workflows
- 🚀 **Owner balance sheet grouped by owner** — property managers and accountants can now see each owner's financial position at a glance. This is critical for owner reporting — one of the most requested features for co-ownership management in Belgian property management
- 🚀 **Detailed owner balance sheet with transactions by property** — owners and accountants can drill down from the owner-level summary into property-level transactions. This provides full financial transparency and eliminates the need to manually cross-reference multiple reports to understand an owner's position across properties
- 📈 **Cross-module scope** — touched both the account module (chart of accounts revert) and the owner balance sheet module (new grouped UI + detail rows), demonstrating broad ownership across the accounting domain
- ⚡ **Independence** — all three items implemented without requiring design handholding, working directly from requirements and UI patterns established in previous dashboard work

## What I Learned
- Reverting a UI decision (moving chart of accounts back) is a valid product choice — sometimes the original location is better because it matches the user's mental model, even if the new location seemed more "logical" from a developer perspective
- Grouping financial data by owner requires careful aggregation logic — each owner may have multiple properties, and the balance sheet must correctly sum across all of them while still allowing drill-down
- The pattern of summary row → expandable detail (transactions by property) is a powerful UI paradigm for financial reports — it lets users stay at the level of detail they need without overwhelming them

## Key Insights
- **User mental models trump developer logic** — moving the chart of accounts into trial balance made sense structurally, but users expected it in the account section. Reverting acknowledges that UX should follow user expectations, not system architecture
- **Owner-centric views are the key to co-ownership accounting** — Belgian property management (syndic) revolves around owners. Every financial view that groups by owner directly serves the core business model
- **Summary → detail drill-down is the right pattern for financial reporting** — accountants need both the big picture (owner totals) and the fine detail (transactions per property). A single view that supports both levels reduces context switching
