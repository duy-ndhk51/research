## Business Impact
- 🚀 **Export feature near completion across 3 report types** (Balance Sheet, Supplier Balance, Owner Balances) — accountants and managers are close to having self-serve financial reporting, eliminating manual data extraction
- 📈 **Pair testing with BA + Customer Support** — validated the export output directly against real business expectations. This catches misalignment early, preventing costly rework after release
- ⚡ **Proactively created backend enhancement task** — didn't just flag the gap; turned it into an actionable task with context from the testing session. This keeps the delivery pipeline moving without waiting for someone else to define the work
- 📈 **Cross-functional collaboration** — working directly with BA and Customer Support shows end-to-end ownership, not just "frontend developer does frontend tasks"

## What I Learned
- Pair testing with BA and Customer Support before finishing the feature is extremely valuable — they see the output through the lens of "what does the business actually need in this file", not "does the code work"
- Export features often have a long tail of small adjustments (column order, formatting, naming, edge cases) that only surface when non-developers see the actual output
- Identifying backend improvements during frontend development demonstrates full-stack awareness — you see the whole picture, not just your layer

## Key Insights
- **Test with real stakeholders early, not just QA** — BA and Customer Support represent the actual users. Their feedback is higher signal than developer self-review for business-facing features like exports
- **Creating the backend task yourself** (instead of just saying "backend needs to fix this") shows initiative and ownership. It reduces friction because the task arrives with full context from someone who understands the problem
- Export PDF/XLSX is a **high-visibility feature** — every stakeholder who downloads a report sees the quality of the work. This is the kind of feature that builds trust with the business side
