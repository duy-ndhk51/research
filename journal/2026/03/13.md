## What I Learned
- Building a reusable component from scratch (Toolbar) took more time than expected — the overhead of designing API, handling edge cases (overflow-x), and following compound pattern rigor is an upfront investment
- The methodology is now internalized: compound component pattern, shadcn philosophy (simple, composable, copy-paste friendly), accessibility decisions (don't add `role` without keyboard behavior), and review-driven refinement
- **Key realization**: time spent on a component that "doesn't bring direct business value" is only wasted if the methodology isn't captured. Since the approach is documented and repeatable, the next component will be built much faster — the investment compounds

## Key Insights
- There's a tension between **building it right** (reusable, well-architected) and **shipping fast** (just make it work). The sweet spot is: build it right the *first time you extract a pattern*, then reap the speed benefits on every subsequent use
- Export PDF/XLSX for financial reports is a high-business-value feature — it's what accountants and managers actually use day-to-day. Prioritizing this kind of work makes the impact visible to stakeholders
- Self-awareness about time allocation is a strength, not a weakness — recognizing the imbalance early means you can course-correct before it becomes a pattern
